# icp-builder — Your dream client

Produces `ICP.md`: a vivid, specific picture of who the coach serves — so the twin can write content that names the client's real fears and copy that actually converts. The sharper this is, the better everything the twin writes. Reusable as the coach's audience clarifies.

## How to run it

One question at a time, by voice. Push for *specific* over *generic* — "busy mums 30-45 who've tried every fad diet" beats "people who want to get fit." Name the real, slightly-private fear. Plain language — say "your dream client", never "ICP" or "avatar."

### The questions
1. **"Describe your dream client — who are they, really?"**
2. **"What's the thing that keeps them up at 2am?"**
3. **"What have they already tried that didn't work?"**
4. **"Under the surface — what do they *actually* want?"** (the real desire beneath the surface goal)
5. **"Where do they hang out, and how do they usually find you?"**
6. **"Who's a bad fit — who do you NOT want?"**

### Probing examples
- "Anyone who wants to train." → "If you could only take one type of person forever, who?"
- Surface desire: "Lose weight." → "And what does losing the weight actually get them — confidence? Their old self back? To stop hiding in photos?"
- 2am fear too tidy: push for the embarrassing, unspoken version.

## Write it
Draft `~/[TwinName]/ICP.md` from `templates/ICP.md`. Fill: who they are, the 2am fear, what they've tried, the deep desire, where to find them, who's a bad fit.

## Read it back
In the coach's cloned voice: **"Here's your dream client as I see them — does this feel like the person you actually want more of?"** Refine until it's unmistakably *their* person.

## Hand off
"That's who we're building for. Last step — let's set the rules for how I work, then I'm live." → proceed to `rules-and-assembly`.
