# Your voice sample — the 2-minute read

**How to record (this matters more than the script):**
- Quiet room. No music, no aircon hum, no mates talking in the background.
- Phone or mic about 20cm from your mouth.
- Talk like you're on the phone to a mate — do NOT "present." Your normal voice is the one we want.
- If you stumble, keep going. Real beats perfect.
- One take, top to bottom. About two minutes. That's it.

---

## The script (read this, naturally)

Righto — here's two minutes of me, talking about what I actually do.

I run my own business. And if you'd told me five years ago how much of my week would disappear into admin, I would've laughed at you. But here's the thing — I love this work. I genuinely do. There's a moment, maybe once a week, where someone I've helped sends me a message out of nowhere, and it reminds me exactly why I started.

Last Wednesday — no, wait, it was Thursday, because I'd just wrapped up the early session — one of my longest-standing clients told me they'd finally hit a goal they'd been chasing for three years. Three years! And you know what got them there in the end? Not some fancy new program. Just showing up, week after week, with someone in their corner.

That's the part of this job nobody can ever take from me.

Now — the boring bits? The chasing, the scheduling, the "just checking in on that invoice" messages I'm writing at ten o'clock at night? Couldn't pay me fifty bucks to say I enjoy those. Couldn't pay me five hundred, honestly.

So here's what I reckon. The work only I can do — the people, the judgment calls, the showing up — that stays mine. Everything else, I'm handing over. Every list, every reminder, every chase-up, every "where are we at with this."

And if you're hearing this in my voice right now… mate, that is genuinely the strangest and best thing I've heard all year.

Alright. Two minutes is up. Let's get to work.
