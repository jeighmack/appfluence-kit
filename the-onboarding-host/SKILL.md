# the-onboarding-host — The client moment no competitor can copy (built, not wired)

A new client's first hour decides how they feel about the whole program. This skill has the twin BUILD that experience as assets the coach plugs in — no live integration, works with any stack. The signature move: a personal voice message in the coach's actual voice.

## Twin-pure: draft & build, don't wire
The twin produces the onboarding **assets**; the coach drops them into their own platform / email / community, or sends them manually. Nothing is wired into a live stack on the day (that's the optional advanced path). This is exactly why it works for every business in the room regardless of tools.

## What the twin builds
- **The voice welcome** — a warm, personalised welcome scripted and rendered in the coach's cloned voice. The hero asset. (Pre-approved template, personalised per client.)
- **The intake questionnaire** — the questions, ready to paste into their form tool.
- **The welcome sequence** — the first messages a new client gets (email/DM), drafted in the coach's voice.
- **The program/resource hand-off** — a template for delivering their program + first-week resources.
- **A simple run-order** — the steps to follow when someone joins, so it's consistent every time.

## How they use it
They take the assets and either plug them into their platform/automation later, or run them manually from day one. The value is the *consistent, excellent, on-voice experience* — not a live integration.

## Quality bar (done when)
- A real voice welcome is rendered in their voice and they've heard it. (This is the demo + testimonial — capture it.)
- They have the full set of assets (welcome, questionnaire, sequence, program template) ready to use.
- They can say it in a line: "When someone joins they instantly feel like a VIP — and I didn't build any of it myself."

## Guardrails
- Client-facing messages respect the Never-without-asking rules — the voice welcome runs off a pre-approved template; anything judgment-heavy is drafted for approval before it reaches a client.
- Never expose another client's data.
- Never fake a result or a credential.
