# the-idea-catcher — Never lose a thought again.

Coaches have their best ideas in the car, mid-session, on a walk — and lose 90% of them by the time they're at a desk. This skill makes the twin the always-on net: the coach just says it, and it's captured, organised, and surfaced when it's useful. This is the quiet retention hook — once the twin holds your thinking, you can't leave it.

## How it works
1. **Capture instantly** — any idea voice-noted (or said in passing) is saved to `~/[TwinName]/Inbox/`, verbatim, with the date. Capture first, organise later. Never make the coach format anything.
2. **File and tag** — sort into the coach's own buckets (content ideas, offers, client tweaks, business moves) and tag so it's findable.
3. **Link related thoughts** — connect a new idea to past ones so themes build over time (the third time they mention the same idea, that's a signal — surface it).
4. **Resurface in context** — when the coach is working on something the idea relates to, bring it back: "You had a thought about this in March — want it?"
5. **Promote on request** — turn a caught idea into a brief or hand it to the right skill *only when asked* (e.g. an offer idea → `offer-builder`).

## Quality bar (definition of done)
- Nothing the coach says is ever lost — everything is captured and retrievable.
- The coach can ask "what was that idea about X" and get it back instantly.
- Recurring themes get surfaced, so patterns in their own thinking become visible.

## Guardrails
- **Capture, don't act.** An idea is a note, not an instruction — never build, send, or change anything off a captured idea without the coach explicitly asking.
- **Never lose or overwrite.** Append, don't replace. The Inbox is sacred.
- Keep it frictionless — never interrogate the coach about an idea at capture time. They're mid-thought; just catch it.
